Leadership isn't a title — it's a practice. In today's rapidly shifting workplace, the ability to develop others has become the single most important skill a leader can possess.

## The Leadership Gap

Organizations everywhere are facing a leadership gap. As experienced leaders retire and workplace dynamics shift, the need for effective leadership development has never been greater. But here's the problem: most leadership training doesn't stick.

Research consistently shows that traditional leadership programs have limited long-term impact. Why? Because they focus on knowledge transfer rather than behavior change.

## What Actually Works

The most effective leadership development happens when:

1. **Learning is applied immediately** — theory without practice fades
2. **Feedback is continuous** — not just annual reviews
3. **Context matters** — what works in one culture may fail in another
4. **Leaders coach, don't just direct** — the shift from manager to coach
